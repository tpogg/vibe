# System Evolution

**Type:** Map of Content
**Domain:** How knowledge systems grow, refactor, and maintain coherence over time

---

## Overview

Knowledge systems are living structures. They grow as new notes are added, refactor as understanding deepens, and risk decay if not maintained. Evolution is not optional — a static knowledge system is a dead one.

## Core Notes

### Growth Patterns
- [[accretion-vs-synthesis]] — adding notes (accretion) is easy; connecting them into arguments (synthesis) is hard and valuable
- [[emergence-thresholds]] — at certain densities, new patterns become visible and synthesis notes become possible
- [[growth-rate-sustainability]] — adding notes faster than they can be linked creates debt; sustainable growth balances input with integration

### Refactoring
- [[note-splitting]] — when atomic notes outgrow their scope, split them and update all incoming links
- [[moc-decomposition]] — when a MOC becomes too large, extract sub-MOCs (as [[agent-cognition]] spawned [[agent-cognition-hooks]] and [[agent-cognition-platforms]])
- [[link-refactoring]] — updating link targets when notes are renamed, split, or merged; maintaining graph integrity

### Maintenance
- [[staleness-detection]] — identifying notes that reference outdated information or broken links
- [[orphan-integration]] — finding and connecting notes that have lost their incoming links
- [[periodic-graph-review]] — scheduled audits of graph health: dead ends, orphans, oversized MOCs, stale content

### Coherence
- [[coherence-as-emergent-property]] — coherence isn't designed; it emerges from consistent linking practices and periodic synthesis
- [[contradiction-detection]] — when notes make conflicting claims, resolution is needed; contradictions are bugs in the knowledge graph
- [[version-history]] — tracking how notes evolved helps understand why current structure exists

## Key Arguments

The primary risk for knowledge systems isn't too little content — it's too much unintegrated content. Accretion without synthesis produces a pile, not a system. See [[accretion-vs-synthesis]] for the detailed argument.

Evolution requires two complementary activities:
1. **Bottom-up growth** — adding new notes, linking them to existing ones
2. **Top-down synthesis** — periodically reviewing clusters, writing synthesis notes, refactoring MOCs

Neither alone is sufficient. Growth without synthesis produces fragmentation. Synthesis without growth produces stagnation.
